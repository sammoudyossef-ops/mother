Couple 💙

Communication tools, trust rebuilding, and the weekly meeting

Weekly couple meeting
Every Sunday, 30 min. No phones. Hot drink. Not a review — a check-in between two builders.
  • 0–5 min: How are we each feeling physically this week?
  • 5–15 min: What do we need from each other this week?
  • 15–25 min: Medical, financial, or baby prep actions due?
  • Close: Name one thing you appreciate about each other
Trust rebuilding — question of the week
Conflict repair protocol
Step 1
Pause — 20 min cool down
Either partner can call pause. No exit in anger. Both agreed to this rule before any conflict.
Step 2
Acknowledge feelings only
She goes first. You listen. Then you. No counter-arguments yet. Feelings, not accusations.
Step 3
Own your part
"What did I contribute to this?" Each person names one thing they'd do differently.
Step 4
One concrete repair action
"Next time X happens, I will do Y." Small, specific, actionable. No sweeping promises.
Step 5
Physical reconnect
A hug before the day ends. Conflict without reconnection leaves residue.
5 emotional safety rules
Rule 1
No important conversations after 10pm or during extreme fatigue
Rule 2
No escalation in public or in front of family — no sides
Rule 3
Her fear of being abandoned is valid. Acknowledge it: "I hear you. I'm not going anywhere."
Rule 4
Your fear of losing yourself is valid. Name it before it becomes resentment.
Rule 5
"I feel unheard" ≠ "You never listen." Use the first form, always.

Shared Vision 🔭

Exercises to build a family culture intentionally, without forcing agreement too fast.

Do one workshop per weekend. The goal is not perfect agreement. The goal is to understand each other before the baby arrives.
Workshop 1 — Family identity
What one word do we want people to feel when they enter our home?
What did we love from our childhood homes, and what do we refuse to repeat?
What traditions do we want weekly, yearly, and for special moments?
What value must our child absolutely grow with?
What do we never want our child to feel?
Workshop 2 — Money & ambition
What does financial security mean to each of us: a number, a feeling, or a lifestyle?
How do we handle the fact that one partner earns more than the other?
What is our rule for debt, savings, and emotional spending?
What is my ambition and how can the family support it instead of competing with it?
What is her ambition and how can I help protect it too?
Workshop 3 — Parenting philosophy
What kind of parent do you want to be? Describe a scene, not an adjective.
What did your parents do that you want to repeat? What do you want to break?
How do we discipline without fear, humiliation, or emotional damage?
What does presence mean after work, when we are tired?
How do we keep the couple alive while becoming parents?
Workshop 4 — Roles & household
Who owns cooking, cleaning, admin, money, appointments, and baby shopping by default?
What tasks drain each of us the most?
How do we ask for help without resentment?
What is the rule when one of us is overwhelmed?
What do we need from grandparents/family, and where are the boundaries?

Medical & Admin 🏥

France: CPAM, CAF, appointments, paternity leave

Organizational guidance only — always verify medical decisions with your doctor or midwife.
CPAM / CAF — urgent actions
  • Get cerfa 15435*02 from doctor/midwife or ameli.fr
  • Send one copy to CPAM (ameli.fr or by post) — before end of June
  • Send one copy to CAF (caf.fr or by post) — before end of June
  • Receive carnet de maternité from doctor
  • Check mutuelle coverage: prenatal, ultrasounds, birth
  • Check CAF PAJE eligibility at caf.fr
  • Register at maternity ward (maternité) — ideally by month 5–6
  • Notify employer of paternity leave (written, 1 month before birth)
  • Birth preparation classes (séances préparation) — register by month 6
  • Post-birth: civil status declaration at mairie within 5 days
  • Post-birth: update CAF for allocation de base PAJE

Anti-overthinking protocol
Step 1
Name it
Write one sentence: "I am overthinking about ___." Externalizing it breaks the loop.
Step 2
Is it actionable?
Can you do something in 48 hours? If yes: do it or schedule it. If no: step 3.
Step 3
Worst / best case
Write both. Reality lives between the extremes. Most fears are neither.
Step 4
Physical reset
10 deep breaths, a walk, 10 minutes of movement. Overthinking lives in a sedentary body.
